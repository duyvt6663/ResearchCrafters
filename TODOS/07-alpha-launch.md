## Quality Questions

- [ ] Did users report a concrete misconception corrected?
- [ ] Can users explain why a failed branch failed?
- [ ] Can users write a claim grounded in evidence?
- [ ] Did experts agree the ERP did not oversimplify the paper?
- [ ] Did users find the setup friction acceptable?
- [ ] Did users share their result voluntarily?

## Launch Readiness

- [ ] Activation and completion metrics are high enough to justify launch.
- [ ] Top confusing stages are fixed.
- [ ] Mentor/evaluator leak tests pass.
- [ ] Runner SLOs are acceptable.
- [ ] Payment flow works.
- [ ] Share cards apply minimum-N suppression.
- [ ] Support and deletion paths exist.

## Metric Targets

These targets ladder up to the PRD success metrics. Treat them as the
quantitative gate for promoting from alpha to public launch.

- [ ] Activation: at least 60% of invited cohort starts the first package.
- [ ] Stage 1 completion: at least 75% of starters finish stage 1 within 24 hours.
- [ ] Stage 3 completion: at least 50% of starters reach stage 3.
- [ ] Stage 5 completion: at least 35% of starters reach stage 5.
- [ ] Full package completion: at least 20% of starters finish the package.
- [ ] Decision pain: at least 40% of learners select a non-canonical branch and
      read its expert feedback at least once.
- [ ] Time in serious effort: median active minutes per package at least 90.
- [ ] Share rate: at least 15% of completers create a share card; at least 5%
      publish it.
- [ ] Willingness to pay: at least 25% conversion among learners who hit the
      first hard branch.

## Cohort Operations

- [ ] Run a weekly check-in with the cohort: written prompt plus opt-in 20-min
      call.
- [ ] Cap waitlist at a known number; do not over-recruit.
- [ ] Set an end date for alpha and communicate it up front.
- [ ] Define a refund policy for paid alpha users: full refund within 14 days
      and pro-rata refund if the package is unpublished due to quality issues.
- [ ] Offer opt-in social proof testimonials with explicit copy approval.
- [ ] Maintain a simple status page or changelog so cohort sees fixes shipping.

## Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] At least 20 users start the flagship package.
- [ ] At least 10 users reach the first hard branch.
- [ ] At least 5 users complete the package or provide high-signal completion feedback.
- [ ] At least 3 users indicate clear willingness to pay.
- [ ] The team can name the next package based on alpha demand.
- [ ] Metric targets above are met or have a clear remediation plan.
